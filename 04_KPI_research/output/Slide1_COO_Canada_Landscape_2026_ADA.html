Cost & Efficiency
Delivering on efficiency ratio commitmentsExecutive Mandate
COOs under board pressure to demonstrate measurable cost improvement — targets set at Investor Days are now active delivery obligations for 2026.
From transformation to execution disciplineSector Trend
Multi-year transformation programs are expected to show productivity returns. COOs are shifting focus from program launch to measurable output and operating leverage.
Cloud migration as a cost leverSector Trend
Workload migration from on-premise to cloud is delivering ~30% cost reduction for institutions that have progressed — now a mainstream efficiency strategy, not a future investment.
🛡
Operational Resilience
OSFI E-21 — full compliance by September 2026Hard Deadline
Institutions must identify critical operations, define impact tolerances, run scenario tests, and document recovery plans. Non-compliance is a regulatory risk, not just a programme gap.
Third-party risk under OSFI B-10Regulatory
Scope expanded beyond outsourcing to all vendor and partner arrangements. Legacy contracts being re-assessed for risk classification, documentation and ongoing monitoring obligations.
Cybersecurity as operational continuityExecutive Mandate
Cybersecurity investment is now framed as an operational continuity issue — COOs co-own it alongside CISOs. Unified security platforms and AI-powered threat detection are the dominant investment theme.
👥
People & Org Change
COO operating model redesignExecutive Mandate
Branch and service centre models are being restructured — away from transactional delivery toward advice and exception-handling. Operational headcount is being redeployed, not just reduced.
Workforce reskilling obligationSector Trend
59% of Canadian C-suite leaders expect many current employee skills to be obsolete by 2030. COOs carry the accountability for managing this transition — including pace, scope, and people impact.
Leadership continuity risk at the COO levelSector Trend
Significant COO-level turnover across Canadian FSI in 2025–2026. Multiple institutions have either new COOs, vacant roles, or restructured operational accountability — increasing execution risk.
⚡
Digital & Payments Infrastructure
Real-Time Rail readiness (RTR)Hard Deadline
Payments Canada targeting Q3 2026 RTR launch. COOs accountable for core system integration, ISO 20022 readiness, and 24/7 operational capability — implementation risk is high for legacy-heavy institutions.
Open Banking — architecture investment nowRegulatory
Consumer-Driven Banking Act (Royal Assent Mar 2026) sets read-access Phase 1 for 2026, write-access Phase 2 for mid-2027. COOs must build API infrastructure ahead of accreditation timelines.
Core system modernisationExecutive Mandate
RTR, Open Banking, and real-time data demands are accelerating the case for core banking replacement or wrapping. COOs managing build-vs-wrap decisions with significant multi-year cost implications.

Cross-sector themes
From Pilots to Value Delivery
AI value targets are now formal board commitments — COOs accountable for translating tech investment into measurable $ productivity and cost reduction
Sector is bifurcating: institutions capturing AI value vs. those still in pilot mode. PwC Canada flags inconsistency in enterprise-scale deployment
Operational Use Cases In Production
Mortgage adjudication, fraud detection, AML monitoring, document processing, and customer service triage are active deployments — not roadmap items
AI-powered processing time reductions measured in hours-to-minutes for credit and compliance workflows
Structural Implications for COOs
AI is being separated from T&O as a standalone function at leading institutions — COO scope is being redefined accordingly
Citi: 54% of finance roles face AI-led displacement risk by 2029 — highest of any major industry. Workforce design is now a core COO responsibility
Gartner: 0% of IT work without AI augmentation by 2030; 25% by AI alone — headcount models require fundamental rethinking
