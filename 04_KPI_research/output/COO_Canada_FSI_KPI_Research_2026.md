## Section 0 — Canadian FSI Market Overview

Canada's financial services sector is one of the most concentrated and stable in the world. Six banks, four life insurers, and a handful of large credit unions and asset managers dominate the market. The federal regulator OSFI supervises all federally incorporated institutions; provincial regulators cover credit unions, securities dealers, and insurance brokers.

### Big 6 Banks — Organization Overview

The Big 6 collectively hold ~90% of all Canadian banking assets. All are publicly traded on the TSX and NYSE.

| Bank | Approx. Total Assets (FY2025) | Primary Focus | Notable 2026 Context |
|---|---|---|---|
| **Royal Bank of Canada (RBC)** | ~$2.2T CAD | Retail, wealth, capital markets, US operations (City National) | Largest Canadian bank by assets; completed HSBC Canada acquisition 2024; created standalone AI Group in early 2026 |
| **Toronto-Dominion Bank (TD)** | ~$2.1T CAD | Retail banking Canada & US, wealth, insurance | US AML/BSA consent order (2024) reshaping US retail strategy; major restructuring underway with $2–2.5B cost reduction program |
| **Bank of Nova Scotia (Scotiabank)** | ~$1.4T CAD | Retail, commercial, international (Latin America) | Refocusing on Canada, US, Mexico; divesting non-core international operations under CEO Scott Thomson |
| **Bank of Montreal (BMO)** | ~$1.4T CAD | Retail, commercial, capital markets, North America | Post Bank of the West integration (2023); efficiency improvement a top 2026 priority |
| **Canadian Imperial Bank of Commerce (CIBC)** | ~$1.0T CAD | Retail, wealth, capital markets | Delivered record Q1 2026 revenue across all segments; strongest efficiency ratio among Big 6 at 51.6% |
| **National Bank of Canada (NBC)** | ~$450B CAD | Retail, commercial, wealth (Quebec-focused) | Completed acquisition of Canadian Western Bank (2024); expanding Western Canada presence |

### Big 4 Life Insurers — COO & Operational Leadership

> Enterprise-level COO titles are less common in the Canadian insurer segment. Operational leadership is typically held by divisional Presidents or EVPs. No named enterprise COO was publicly identifiable for any of the Big 4 as of May 2026.

### Other Significant Players

- **Credit unions & caisses populaires** — provincially regulated; Desjardins (QC) is the largest with ~$430B in assets, comparable to NBC in scale. BC's Vancity and Coast Capital are notable in Western Canada.
- **Payments Canada** — operator of Canada's core payment systems (LVTS, ACSS, and the incoming Real-Time Rail). COOs across the sector are directly impacted by its infrastructure roadmap.
- **Regulators shaping COO agendas in 2026:** OSFI (prudential), Bank of Canada (monetary policy + consumer-driven banking framework), FINTRAC (AML/CFT), FCAC (consumer protection — transitioning oversight to Bank of Canada under new open banking framework).

## How to Read This Report

Two types of content are distinguished throughout:

- **COMMITTED** — formally published targets from investor days, earnings releases, SEC filings, regulatory compliance deadlines. These are measurable commitments with named owners and stated timelines.
- **DIRECTION** — themes and ambitions cited in industry reports, conference presentations, analyst commentary, or strategic narratives. Not yet operationalized as KPIs/OKRs.

## Section 2 — Strategic Ambitions & Directions for 2026

> These are themes prominently cited in 2025–2026 strategic communications, industry research, and executive speeches. They reflect directional intent — **not yet formalized as KPIs/OKRs** with published targets.

### 2.1 From Transformation to Execution Discipline

The dominant shift in 2026 COO narrative across Canadian FSI is a move away from broad transformation programs toward disciplined execution: delivery of promised cost reductions, tech investment ROI, and operating model simplification.

- TD has explicitly framed its strategy as building a "simpler, faster, more efficient" bank.
- Massey Henry's 2026 Canadian executive priorities survey identifies "execution, productivity, and disciplined decision-making" as the most critical leadership capability — replacing last year's "transformation leadership."
- COOs are being held accountable for translating multi-year tech investments (AI, cloud) into measurable productivity outputs, not just deployment.

### 2.2 AI Integration into Core Operations

Banks are actively embedding AI into operational workflows, but specific workforce KPIs are not yet publicly committed:

- Automated document processing, fraud detection, and customer service triage are in broad deployment.
- AI-augmented mortgage approvals, credit adjudication, and AML monitoring are cited as 2026 priorities.
- PwC Canada notes AI is delivering results "where it is" — but highlights inconsistency: many banks are still in pilot mode with no enterprise-scale productivity targets disclosed.
- RBC created a dedicated AI Group (Q1 2026), with Bruce Ross transitioning from Group Head Technology & Operations to lead it — a structural signal of operational AI being treated as its own function.

### 2.3 Operating Model Redesign

- COOs are redesigning branch and service-center models (TD: evolving branches into "advice centers").
- Workforce restructuring linked to AI is occurring globally in FSI, but Canadian bank COOs have not yet published headcount reduction targets for 2026. International comparisons: HSBC explored 10% workforce reduction via AI-driven transformation (disclosed in 2026).
- Operational resilience and third-party vendor consolidation are active workstreams under OSFI B-10.

### 2.4 Cybersecurity as Operational Continuity

- Capgemini Top Trends 2026 identifies unified security platforms and AI-powered threat detection as top operational investment priorities.
- Canadian FSI IT spending trends: Cyber, cloud, and compliance are the top three IT investment categories (Omega Systems, 2026).
- Not yet formalized as COO-owned KPIs in public disclosures — typically sits under CRO/CISO joint ownership.

### 2.5 Open Banking & Payments Readiness

- The Consumer-Driven Banking Act received Royal Assent (March 26, 2026), but implementation is still in early stages — a 2026 Phase 1 launch is widely seen as at risk.
- Real-Time Rail is in industry testing (Q1 2026); COOs are managing integration complexity with legacy core systems.
- This is a direction, not yet a committed operational KPI — institutions are investing in readiness but not publishing specific compliance milestones beyond regulatory requirements.
